## Introduction to Calculus

While computers are inherently discrete, they frequently model and interact with the continuous physical world. Calculus provides the mathematical tools to understand rates of change and accumulation.

### Core Topics

1.  **Differential Calculus**: Understanding derivatives as rates of change. Crucial for optimization algorithms like Gradient Descent used in training neural networks.
2.  **Integral Calculus**: Calculating accumulation and areas. Used in physics engines and probability (calculating expected values for continuous distributions).
3.  **Multivariable Calculus**: Extending calculus to multiple dimensions, which is essential for machine learning models dealing with many parameters.
