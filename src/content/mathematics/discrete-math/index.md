## Introduction to Discrete Mathematics

Discrete mathematics is the foundation of computer science. Unlike continuous mathematics (like calculus), discrete math deals with distinct, separated values. This makes it the perfect mathematical language for computers, which operate on discrete binary states.

### Core Topics

1.  **Logic and Proofs**: The foundation of valid reasoning. Essential for writing correct programs, understanding algorithms, and formal verification.
2.  **Set Theory**: The mathematical science of collections of objects. Sets are ubiquitous in database theory and programming languages.
3.  **Combinatorics**: The study of counting, arranging, and combining objects. Crucial for analyzing the complexity of algorithms.
4.  **Graph Theory**: The study of networks of connected nodes. Used extensively in networking, routing, state machines, and social networks.
